# Control-plane legitimacy

## Why this document exists

The repository already modeled delegation, evidence, registry governance, and legitimacy-adjacent controls. The new essay set adds a missing surface: what happens when real authority has already migrated into infrastructure, AI intermediaries, and licensing coalitions.

## Core additions

### Authority relocation

`patterns/control-plane-shift-detection` records when a system stops merely implementing policy and starts functioning as the effective site of governance.

### Legitimacy gap evaluation

`patterns/legitimacy-gap-evaluator` distinguishes technical enforceability from legitimate control by scoring contestability, revocation, auditability, and binding effect.

### Intermediary governance

`overlays/intermediary-governance-overlay` treats intermediaries as governance actors with provenance, disclosure, and challenge obligations.

### Knowledge substrate integrity

`profiles/knowledge-substrate-integrity-profile` preserves provenance, attribution, and policy binding across transformed knowledge outputs.

### Governed licensing

`profiles/governed-license-authority` and `profiles/coalition-legitimacy-model` model who may issue ecosystem licenses and what legitimacy conditions prevent coalition capture.

## Design stance

These packages do not treat legitimacy as commentary. They treat it as executable governance: inspectable structure, explicit controls, and evidence-bearing decisions.
