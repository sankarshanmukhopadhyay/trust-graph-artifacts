# High-risk digital infrastructure governance

## Purpose

High-risk digital infrastructure is defined by consequence intensity, systemic coupling, interpretive authority, and dependency lock-in. A system may become high risk because of what it does, where it sits, who depends on it, or how hard it is to exit.

v0.3.0 adds a governance profile and evidence model for systems that require governance inside the architecture rather than oversight beside it.

## Package surface

- `profiles/high-risk-digital-infrastructure-governance-profile`
- `evidence/high-risk-governance-assurance-record`
- `overlays/constitutional-digital-infrastructure-controls`

## Core controls

- risk classification
- traceability
- reversibility
- delegation boundaries
- guardrail logic
- contestability
- rate and scope limits
- governance logs
- public transparency
- independent auditability
- remediation paths

## Assurance posture

High-risk governance cannot be satisfied by policy prose alone. A verifier should be able to test whether the system can reconstruct a decision, halt or reverse a cascading effect, enforce boundary conditions, prove audit coverage, and route affected parties into meaningful contestability.
