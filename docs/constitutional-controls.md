# Constitutional digital infrastructure controls

## Purpose

Constitutional controls are non-derogable constraints for high-risk digital infrastructure. They define what the system may not automate, infer, hide, or make irreversible even when ordinary policy rules would allow execution.

The overlay is designed for systems that affect rights, access, eligibility, livelihood, mobility, institutional standing, or essential services.

## Package surface

- `overlays/constitutional-digital-infrastructure-controls`
- `profiles/high-risk-digital-infrastructure-governance-profile`
- `evidence/high-risk-governance-assurance-record`

## Non-derogable control classes

- right to context
- right to contestability
- right to reversibility
- right to explanation
- right to human authority in protected decision classes
- prohibition on protected or inadmissible inference categories
- independent audit access
- public transparency for material control changes

## Assurance posture

The constitutional overlay gives assurance teams a boundary test: even if a model, agent, policy engine, or registry can technically execute an operation, it must be denied or escalated when the operation crosses a non-derogable governance boundary.
